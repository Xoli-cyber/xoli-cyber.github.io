Cde Zama Mnyabiso - Deputy General Secretary

Cde Zama Mnyabiso, a young activist born in the 90s at Chris Hani Baragwanath Hospital in Johannesburg, relocated to East London in the Eastern Cape. He began his activism within the African National Congress at the Malcomas Magabela Branch, Ward 32 (East London), and joined the South African Students Congress at Border Technikon (Walter Sisulu University).

Upon employment, he joined NEHAWU and served as a shop steward for the Buffalo City Metro Health District. His leadership led to his election as branch secretary of East London Clinics and later as part of the Regional Executive Committee of Yure Mdyogolo Region (NEHAWU).

In 2023, he was elected Chairperson of COSATU Young Workers, East London Local, and served as a PEC member (2023–2025). He is currently the Regional Deputy Secretary of Buffalo City Metro Municipality South African Youth Council and Interim Deputy General Secretary of NDASWU.

Cde Zolani M Masoleng - General Secretary

Zolani Matthews Masoleng is a seasoned legal and technical professional with over 25 years of experience spanning philosophy, labour law, conflict resolution, nuclear manufacturing, and metrology. He is pursuing a Master of Laws in Labour Law at UNISA.

Professionally, he serves as Chief Technical Officer at NECSA, specialising in quality compliance in advanced nuclear manufacturing. He has held leadership roles in SANDF, managing SANAS-accredited metrology labs.

In the labour movement, he has served as Regional Secretary of NEHAWU Tshwane, representing over 35,500 members. Zolani also co-founded CRACOMI and held youth leadership positions in SASCO and community initiatives. His expertise combines law, technology, and unionism for workplace justice and transformation.

Cde Gugulethu Mthombeni - Deputy President

Gugulethu Mthombeni, born in KwaZulu-Natal, works as an admin assistant at the Gauteng Department of Education. She holds a diploma in Human Resources and certificates in leadership, diversity, ICT, accounting, and management.

She has long-standing experience in the trade union and political landscape — serving in ANC, SACP, and NEHAWU structures, including roles as branch chairperson, COSATU Tembisa Local Deputy Chairperson, and NEHAWU Provincial Treasurer.

She has extensive negotiation experience at GDE, PSBF, and CCPGP and holds qualifications in Finance and Investment from Wits University. Currently, she serves as Interim Deputy President of NDASWU, advocating for equity, professionalism, and empowerment in the labour movement.
